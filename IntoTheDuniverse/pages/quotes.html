Dune Chapter Quotes

At the start of each chapter begeins a quote from some person, some book, or some other source that hints at the direction and themes of the ensuing chapter. This page here is meant to act as a chapterhouse of all the beginning chapter quotes of the book DUNE.

Chapter 1

“A beginning is the time for taking the most delicate care that the balances are correct. This every sister of the Bene Gesserit knows. To begin your study of the life of Maud'Dib, then, take care that you first place him in his time: born in the 57th year of the Padishah Emperor, Shaddam IV. And take the most special care that you locate Maud'Dib in his place: the planet Arrakis. Do not be deceived by the fact that he was born on Caladan and lived his first fifteen years there. Arrakis, the planet known as Dune, is forever his place.”

—from "Manual of Muad'Dib" by the Princess Irulan

Chapter 2

“To attempt an understanding of Muad’Dib without understanding his mortal enemies, the Harkonnens, is to attempt seeing Truth without knowing Falsehood. It is the attempt to see the Light without knowing Darkness. It cannot be.

—from "Manual of Muad'Dib" by the Princess Irulan

Chapter 3

“Thus spoke St. Alia-of-the-Knife: “The Reverend Mother must combine the seductive wiles of a courtesan with the untouchable majesty of a virgin goddess, holding these attributes in tension so long as the powers of her youth endure. For when youth and beauty have gone, she will find that the place-between, once occupied by tension, has become a wellspring of cunning and resourcefulness.”

—from "Muad'Dib: Family Commentaries" by the Princess Irulan
